Harriet Tubman

An African American women who escaped enslavement helps other enslaved African Americans escape.

Dorchester County, Maryland

Harriet was born in March 1822 into a enslaved family. Harriet escaped out of enslavement. She went on 13 trips to help roughly 70 people escape enslavement too. During the American Civil War she served as a armed scout and spy. She also served in the Union Army. As a child she was beaten due to being enslaved.

Timeline of Harriet Tubmans life

  • March 1822- Harriet was born
  • September 1849- Harriet escaped Philadelphia
  • In 1858- she met John Brown and helped him on a raid.
  • March 10, 1913- Harriet sadly died.

    • "I had crossed the line. I was free; but there was no one to welcome me to the land of freedom. I was a stranger in a strange land"

       -Harriet Tubman
